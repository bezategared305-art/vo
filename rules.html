Voting Eligibility & Rules

Official guidelines for voter eligibility and election regulations

Voter Eligibility

Who can vote in this election

Age Requirement

Must be 18 years or older on election day. Proof of age may be required.

Citizenship

Must be a registered citizen of the country. Dual citizens may vote in national elections only.

Residency

Must be a resident of the electoral district for at least 30 days before registration.

Felony Conviction

Individuals currently serving felony sentences are ineligible to vote until sentence completion.

Mental Competency

Must be of sound mind and not under legal guardianship for voting rights.

Registration Status

Must be registered to vote at least 15 days before the election date.

Voting Rules

Regulations for casting your vote

1

One Vote Per Person

Each registered voter may cast only one ballot per election. Multiple voting is illegal.

2

Secret Ballot

All votes are confidential and anonymous. No one can trace your vote back to you.

3

No Electioneering

Campaigning or discussing candidates within voting areas is strictly prohibited.

4

Valid Identification

Government-issued ID must be presented before receiving a ballot.

5

Time Limit

You have 10 minutes to complete your ballot once you begin voting.

6

No Assistance

You must vote independently unless you have registered for assisted voting.

Important Notice

Violation of voting rules may result in:

  • Immediate disqualification of your ballot
  • Legal prosecution under Election Fraud Act
  • Fines up to $10,000 and/or imprisonment
  • Loss of voting rights for future elections
  • Criminal record for election offenses

If you witness any suspicious activity, report immediately to election officials.
